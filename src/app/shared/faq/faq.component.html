A home warranty is different from homeowners insurance. Homeowners insurance covers acts of God and natural disasters, such as fires and floods. This is usually mandated by a mortgage provider. Insurance is also regulated at state and federal levels.

What insurance doesn’t cover is the basic wear and tear on appliances and systems in your homes. That’s where home warranties come in. Do I need both home warranty and homeowners insurance?

Homeowners insurance covers acts of nature and disasters. For example, if there is a severe weather event that causes damage to your home, that damage should be covered by homeowners insurance. Insurance is regulated by the government and is usually required by law as part of a title.

In contrast, home warranties cover systems and appliances in the home. These are things not covered by homeowners insurance. Once the manufacturer warranty on an air conditioning or washer/dryer has run out, a home warranty fills in the gap, protecting homeowners from costly repairs or replacements. Insurance and a home warranty are two different services, both of which are needed if you are going to be fully covered.

A home warranty is different from homeowners insurance. Homeowners insurance covers acts of God and natural disasters, such as fires and floods. This is usually mandated by a mortgage provider. Insurance is also regulated at state and federal levels.

What insurance doesn’t cover is the basic wear and tear on appliances and systems in your homes. That’s where home warranties come in. Do I need both home warranty and homeowners insurance?

Homeowners insurance covers acts of nature and disasters. For example, if there is a severe weather event that causes damage to your home, that damage should be covered by homeowners insurance. Insurance is regulated by the government and is usually required by law as part of a title.

In contrast, home warranties cover systems and appliances in the home. These are things not covered by homeowners insurance. Once the manufacturer warranty on an air conditioning or washer/dryer has run out, a home warranty fills in the gap, protecting homeowners from costly repairs or replacements. Insurance and a home warranty are two different services, both of which are needed if you are going to be fully covered.
