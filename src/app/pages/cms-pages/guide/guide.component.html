Home Warranties vs. Homeowners Insurance

Home warranties are not the same as homeowners insurance. Many times, homeowners insurance is required by law by your lender. Insurance covers natural disasters, fires and floods. If anything like this occurs, your insurance policy (regulated by the state and federal government) will provide financial assistance according to stated terms

A home warranty is an optional agreement. These are regulated differently by each state. Companies that offer home warranties can offer different levels of coverage and different terms and conditions. You want to find a home warranty company that has clear contracts and covers the items in your home that are no longer under manufacturer warranty

Home warranties protect homeowners from the enormous cost of repairing or replacing items that break down due to wear and tear. Appliances (like a washer/dryer, hot water heater or fridge) and systems (electrical, plumbing, HVAC) are essential to the function or your home and very costly to fix or replace. A home warranty service agreement gives homeowners access to a vast network of qualified service providers and peace of mind if a breakdown occurs

Elite Home Warranty: The Basics

Home warranties provide coverage for the major systems and appliances in your home. When you first purchase a new HVAC unit or washer/dryer set, it will be covered for a short time by the manufacturer. If it breaks down in the first year or two, you’ll be able to get payment from the manufacturer to cover that cost. This is great and assures you that if it breaks down even while it’s new, you won’t be stuck with a major repair or replacement bill. But what happens once that manufacturer warranty has expired?

What happens is that you, as a homeowner, are stuck with the full cost of repairing or replacing a broken down item in your home. While a microwave may not be very costly, consider what you would do if your furnace broke down? What about a major plumbing issue? All of these unknowns can cause a lot of uncertainty. Most people’s budgets aren’t prepared to handle costly breakdowns. To offset this serious concern, many people turn to home warranties

Home warranty service agreements exist as a line of protection against unexpected repairs or replacements. Once the items in your home have aged out of a manufacturer warranty, you can buy a home warranty plan. These plans and policies are pretty simple. Typically, you will pay an annual fee and then receive a certain level of financial security should a covered item break down

 How is a home warranty different from homeowners insurance?

A home warranty is different from homeowners insurance. Homeowners insurance covers acts of God and natural disasters, such as fires and floods. This is usually mandated by a mortgage provider. Insurance is also regulated at state and federal levels.

What insurance doesn’t cover is the basic wear and tear on appliances and systems in your homes. That’s where home warranties come in. A home warranty plan can cover your major home systems (such as HVAC, electrical and plumbing) as well as a selection of home appliances (such as refrigerators, oven, washer/ dryer, etc.). This provides another layer of support and protects a homeowner from having to pay enormous costs for repairs and replacements in the home. Elite Home Warranty is the premiere provider of plans like these, partnering with homeowners and offering excellent coverage. Contact us to learn more.

 Do I need both home warranty and homeowners insurance?

Homeowners insurance covers acts of nature and disasters. For example, if there is a severe weather event that causes damage to your home, that damage should be covered by homeowner’s insurance. Insurance is regulated by the government and is usually required by law as part of a title.

In contrast, home warranties cover systems and appliances in the home. These are things not covered by homeowners insurance. Once the manufacturer warranty on an air conditioning or washer/dryer has run out, a home warranty fills in the gap, protecting homeowners from costly repairs or replacements. Insurance and a home warranty are two different services, both of which are needed if you are going to be fully covered.
